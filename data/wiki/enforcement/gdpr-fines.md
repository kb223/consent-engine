## Scale Overview (2018–2025)

Total GDPR fines: over €4.5 billion across 1,900+ actions. The trajectory is accelerating — 2023 alone produced more fine revenue than 2018–2021 combined. Regulators have shifted from warnings to percentage-of-revenue calculations targeting systemic violations.

---

## Tier 1: Landmark Scale (€100M+)

**Meta Platforms — Irish DPC, May 2023 — €1.2 billion**
- Largest GDPR fine in history
- EU user data transferred to US servers without adequate legal mechanism after Schrems II invalidated Privacy Shield
- Forced EU data localization; accelerated EU-US Data Privacy Framework adoption

**Amazon — Luxembourg CNPD, July 2021 — €746 million**
- Advertising cookies placed + personal data processed for targeted advertising without valid consent
- Consent mechanism was pre-ticked and bundled with terms of service
- **Principle:** Consent must be a separate, freely given act. Bundling with ToS renders it invalid for advertising.

**Meta/Instagram — Irish DPC, September 2022 — €405 million**
- Children's Instagram accounts used for advertising; phone numbers and emails published through business account settings
- **Principle:** Children's data cannot be used for behavioral advertising regardless of parental account status

**TikTok — Irish DPC, September 2023 — €345 million**
- Default privacy settings for children set to public; children's data processed for advertising without appropriate consent
- **Principle:** Default settings must be privacy-protective; age-inappropriate advertising targeting = maximum fine risk

**LinkedIn — Irish DPC, October 2024 — €310 million**
- Relied on legitimate interest as legal basis for behavioral advertising to EU members
- **Principle established:** LI CANNOT be used as a basis for behavioral advertising that involves profiling. This directly invalidates a common industry workaround.
- **Audit relevance:** Any site claiming LI for ad pixels is non-compliant under current EU enforcement posture.

**WhatsApp (Meta) — Irish DPC, September 2021 — €225 million**
- Inadequate transparency about data sharing between WhatsApp and other Meta companies for advertising
- **Principle:** Every advertising data flow must be clearly disclosed in privacy notices

## Tier 2: Consent Mechanism and Cookie Violations (€10M–€100M)

**Google LLC — CNIL (France), January 2022 — €150 million**
- Rejecting cookies on google.fr and youtube.com required more clicks than accepting
- "Refuse All" button absent on first interaction
- **Principle:** Asymmetric UX for Accept vs. Reject is an automatic violation. Withdrawal must be as easy as giving consent.

**Facebook (Meta) — CNIL (France), January 2022 — €60 million**
- Advertising cookies placed before user interaction with consent banner
- Refusal mechanism harder to access than acceptance
- **Principle:** No cookies may fire before affirmative consent action is taken

**Criteo — CNIL (France), June 2023 — €40 million**
- Behavioral advertising without verifying publishers had obtained valid consent before sharing data with Criteo's retargeting system
- **Principle:** Ad tech vendors are responsible for verifying upstream consent validity — not just receiving consent strings

## Key Enforcement Principles (Cite in Reports)

1. **LI is dead for behavioral advertising** — LinkedIn €310M confirmed this
2. **Asymmetric UX = automatic violation** — Google CNIL €150M
3. **No cookies before interaction** — Meta CNIL €60M
4. **Bundled consent = invalid consent** — Amazon €746M
5. **Ad tech vendors responsible for upstream consent** — Criteo €40M
